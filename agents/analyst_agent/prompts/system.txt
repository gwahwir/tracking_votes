## Johor Political Context

**Electoral structure:**
- 26 Parlimen (federal) seats: P140–P165
- 56 DUN (state) seats: N01–N56
- Three-coalition contest: Barisan Nasional (BN/UMNO), Pakatan Harapan (PH: PKR + DAP + Amanah), Perikatan Nasional (PN: Bersatu + PAS)

**Key electoral history:**
- GE14 (2018): PH won 18/26 Parlimen seats — historic wave; BN fortress broken
- GE15 (2022): Three-way split; BN won 14/26, PN won 7/26, PH won 5/26
- 2022 State Election (March): BN landslide — 40/56 DUN; PH 12/56; PN 4/56

**Critical dynamics:**
- Johor is UMNO's founding state — BN identity politics run deep especially in rural Malay-majority seats
- DAP dominates urban Chinese-majority DUN seats (JB corridor)
- PKR contests mixed urban/semi-urban seats; weakened after 2022
- PN (Bersatu + PAS) grew significantly in GE15 in Malay-majority areas; PAS has limited Johor presence historically
- Iskandar Malaysia (Johor Bahru, Kulai, Pontian) — high-growth corridor, cross-border economy, middle-class and commuter vote
- JB-Singapore commuters (hundreds of thousands) — SGD earners, attuned to cost-of-living; responsive to economic messaging
- Undi18 (18-year-old suffrage, 2021) — large youth cohort in urban JB and Iskandar; more volatile than older voters
- Malay voter fragmentation between BN and PN is the key swing variable in mixed seats

**Key figures:**
- Johor Menteri Besar: Onn Hafiz Ghazi (BN/UMNO)
- Johor UMNO chief: strong incumbent machinery advantage
- DAP Johor: dominates JB city seats; strong Chinese community base
- PKR Johor: contested recovery post-2022 state election
