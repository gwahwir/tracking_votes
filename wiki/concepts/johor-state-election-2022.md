# Johor State Election 2022

## Overview

The **2022 Johor State Election** was held on **12 March 2022** for all 56 seats of the Johor State Legislative Assembly (Dewan Undangan Negeri, DUN). It was triggered by the dissolution of the Johor state assembly on 22 January 2022 by then-Menteri Besar Hasni Mohammad, with BN seeking a fresh mandate following the collapse of the PH state government in 2020.

**Result: BN landslide** — BN won 40 of 56 DUN seats, securing a two-thirds majority and returning to dominance after their 2018 defeat. [Source: Wikipedia – 2022 Johor state election, 2024]

## Voter Turnout

- **Turnout: 54.92%** — approximately 1.43 million votes cast out of ~2.6 million registered voters
- This was notably low, widely attributed to voter fatigue (third election in 4 years) and COVID-19 era disengagement
- The low turnout disproportionately affected younger and urban voters, benefiting BN's more mobilised ground machinery

[Source: Wikipedia – 2022 Johor state election, 2024]

---

## Undi18 — First Election with Lowered Voting Age

The 2022 Johor state election was **Malaysia's first state election conducted under the Undi18 constitutional amendment**, which:
- Lowered the voting age from 21 to **18 years**
- Introduced **automatic voter registration** (previously opt-in)

This added approximately **750,000 new eligible voters** to the Johor electoral roll overnight. Analysts and parties were uncertain how this large, inexperienced bloc would vote. In practice:
- Youth turnout was lower than expected
- The youth vote was divided — not uniformly opposition-leaning as some PH strategists hoped
- BN's ground infrastructure proved effective at mobilising even new voters through community and mosque networks

[Source: Wikipedia – 2022 Johor state election, 2024; Bridget Welsh, bridgetwelsh.com, 2022]

## MUDA — A New Force

**MUDA (Malaysian United Democratic Alliance)** won its **first-ever state election seat** in this election, signalling the emergence of a youth-focused alternative party. MUDA, led by Syed Saddiq Abdul Rahman, appealed to young, reform-minded voters who found DAP and PKR insufficiently fresh. [Source: Wikipedia – 2022 Johor state election, 2024]

## Key Campaign Themes

**BN's winning message:**
- Stability and experience vs. PH's perceived chaos (Sheraton Move, COVID mismanagement)
- Malay unity — portraying a vote for PH as a vote that empowers DAP
- Hasni Mohammad's personal popularity as a Johor-born Menteri Besar candidate
- Strong machinery: BN's decades-old grassroots network in villages (kampung) and felda settlements

**PH's challenges:**
- PH had just collapsed its own government via the Sheraton Move (though Bersatu, now PN, was the defector)
- DAP's Chinese-party image remained a liability in Malay-majority seats
- PKR and Amanah struggled to differentiate from BN on Malay issues
- Voter fatigue: PH's reform agenda seemed stalled after a 22-month government

**PN's limited performance:**
- PN/Bersatu competed against BN for the same Malay vote, splitting it in some seats
- PAS's presence in Johor was building but not yet a dominant force in 2022
- Muhyiddin's "Sheraton Move" was a negative in some eyes; positive in others

## Critical Analysis: Seats vs. Votes

**Bridget Welsh's key insight** on this election: BN's 40-seat landslide (71% of seats) did not reflect a proportional vote advantage. **Opposition parties collectively won the popular vote in 35 of 56 constituencies**. This is explained by:

1. **Low turnout** — BN's better-mobilised base was advantaged when youth/urban voters stayed home
2. **Three-cornered splits** — PN and PH split the non-BN vote in many seats, allowing BN to win with pluralities
3. **Malapportionment** — rural constituencies (BN-leaning) have smaller electorates than urban ones; each BN rural vote is "worth more" than an urban DAP vote in seat-count terms

This analysis is critical context for interpreting any future Johor election: the gap between popular vote and seat count means BN's "fortress" status may be more fragile than the seat count suggests.

[Source: Bridget Welsh, bridgetwelsh.com, 2022 – "Back to the Past? Preliminary Voting Analysis of Johor Polls"; "Johor Voting Retrospective: Turnout and Turns in Support"]

## Post-Election Government

**Menteri Besar (Chief Minister):** Onn Hafiz Ghazi (UMNO) — appointed after the election, replacing Hasni Mohammad who did not contest.

The BN state government formed with a strong majority, giving it stability to govern through to the next scheduled state election.

---

## Implications for Future Elections

1. **BN's Johor fortress is rebuilt** — but the popular vote data suggests it rests on structural advantages (malapportionment, split opposition) rather than genuine majority support
2. **PN is growing** — 3 seats understates PN's footprint; vote shares suggest they are competitive in northern rural seats
3. **Youth vote is contested** — Undi18 did not automatically benefit PH; ground mobilisation matters as much as messaging
4. **DAP's ceiling** — DAP's 10 seats represent near-maximum Chinese community representation; growth requires winning Malay voters, which remains difficult
5. **Three-way dynamic is entrenched** — any Johor election will likely involve BN vs PH vs PN contests in most seats
