# GE14 (2018) and GE15 (2022) — Johor Federal Results

## Overview

Johor has 26 **Parlimen (federal parliamentary)** seats. The two most recent general elections produced dramatically different outcomes for Johor — GE14 was a historic opposition wave, while GE15 saw a three-way split that returned BN to dominance while PN grew significantly.

---

## GE14 — 9 May 2018

### National Context

GE14 was Malaysia's most consequential election in 61 years. Pakatan Harapan (PH), led by 92-year-old former PM Mahathir Mohamad, defeated UMNO/BN for the first time since independence. The catalyst was public outrage over the **1MDB corruption scandal** implicating then-PM Najib Razak. [Source: Wikipedia – 2018 Malaysian general election, 2024]

**National result:**
- PH: 121 seats (majority)
- BN: 79 seats (historic low)
- PAS: 18 seats
- Others/GPS: 18 seats

### Johor in GE14

Johor — historically BN's "fortress" — fell to Pakatan Harapan in both federal and state contests simultaneously.

**Johor Parlimen result (26 seats, approximate):**

| Coalition | Approx. Seats Won |
|-----------|------------------|
| Pakatan Harapan (PH) | ~18–20 seats |
| Barisan Nasional (BN) | ~6–8 seats |
| Others | ~0–1 |

**Key factors in Johor GE14:**
- **Anti-Najib / 1MDB sentiment** was particularly strong in urban Johor where educated middle class voters were energised
- **Chinese voter swing** to DAP was near-total in JB urban constituencies
- **Malay vote split** between BN and PAS weakened BN in semi-urban constituencies
- Johor's proximity to Singapore meant the 1MDB-related international embarrassment was acutely felt

**PH won the Johor state government**, installing a PH Menteri Besar (Chief Minister) for the first time. [Source: Wikipedia – 2018 Malaysian general election, 2024]

---

## Post-GE14 Johor Instability (2020–2022)

The PH Johor state government was relatively short-lived:

1. **February 2020** — The "Sheraton Move" political crisis collapsed the PH federal government nationally. Bersatu under Muhyiddin defected from PH.
2. **March 2020** — A state-level political crisis led to the collapse of the Johor PH state government. A new BN-led state government was formed without an election.
3. **January 2022** — The Johor state assembly was dissolved, triggering the **2022 Johor state election** (March 2022), which BN won decisively.

## GE15 — 19 November 2022

### National Context

GE15 produced Malaysia's first **hung parliament**. Three major blocs emerged:
- **PH**: 82 seats (Anwar Ibrahim's bloc)
- **PN** (Muhyiddin/PAS): 73 seats
- **BN**: 30 seats (historic low)
- **GPS** (Sarawak): 23 seats

After a constitutional crisis, the **National Unity Government** was formed with Anwar Ibrahim as PM, supported by PH, BN (UMNO), GPS, and smaller parties. PN became the main opposition. [Source: Wikipedia – 2022 Malaysian general election, 2024]

### Johor in GE15

Johor held its state election separately in March 2022 (before GE15). For the November 2022 GE15 federal election, Johor again had three-way contests in most seats.

**Johor GE15 Parlimen result (26 seats, approximate):**

| Coalition | Approx. Seats Won |
|-----------|------------------|
| Barisan Nasional (BN) | ~12–15 seats |
| Pakatan Harapan (PH) | ~7–9 seats |
| Perikatan Nasional (PN) | ~2–4 seats |

**Key factors in Johor GE15:**
- **Three-cornered contests** in most seats; Malay vote split between BN and PN hurt both in some constituencies but BN generally benefited from incumbency and resources
- **PN "green wave"** — PAS/Bersatu performed significantly better than GE14, particularly in rural northern Johor
- **Malay voter behaviour**: Some Malay voters switched from PH to PN due to objections to DAP's influence in PH
- **Chinese voter bloc** remained solidly PH/DAP; critical in determining margins in urban seats
- **Undi18 youth voters** participating in a federal election for the first time; their impact was mixed — some youth voted PN, others PH

### DAP's Johor Performance (GE15)

DAP retained its core Johor Parlimen seats:
- P156 Labis
- P158 Bakri
- P162 Kluang
- P165 Iskandar Puteri
- P166 Kulai

## Analytical Notes (Bridget Welsh Framework)

Welsh's analysis of the 2022 Johor state election — which preceded GE15 — highlighted:
- BN's dominant 40-seat majority masked the fact that **opposition parties collectively won the popular vote in 35 of 56 constituencies**
- This "electoral distortion" is critical context for interpreting Johor seat counts — a landslide in seats does not always reflect a proportional vote share advantage
- **Youth voter (Undi18) turnout** in 2022 state election was the first test of the expanded electorate; results showed youth voters were divided, not uniformly opposition-leaning
- **Women candidates** remain underrepresented in Johor across all parties

[Source: Bridget Welsh, bridgetwelsh.com, 2022 – "Back to the Past? Preliminary Voting Analysis of Johor Polls"]
