# Johor Bahru – Singapore Relations

## Overview

Johor Bahru (JB) and Singapore share a 1.056km land border across the Straits of Johor, connected by the **Johor‑Singapore Causeway**. The bilateral relationship is uniquely intensive: hundreds of thousands of people cross daily, a significant portion of Johor's working population earns in Singapore dollars, and the two governments are jointly developing the **Johor‑Singapore Special Economic Zone (JS‑SEZ)**. Understanding this relationship is essential for interpreting Johor electoral politics.

---

## The Johor‑Singapore Causeway

**Physical specifications:**
- Length: 1.056 km
- Carries: motor vehicles, bicycles, pedestrian walkway, and the KTM Shuttle Tebrau rail service
- Built: 1923; the primary land crossing since independence

**Traffic volume:**
- ~350,000 daily travellers (typical range: 250,000–400,000)
- **Peak traffic: 510,000 crossings** on 28 March 2024 — the highest ever recorded at Singapore's land borders
- The Causeway is one of the world's busiest land border crossings

**Dual function:**
- Transportation corridor for commuters, goods, and tourism
- Carries a water pipeline supplying Singapore under the 1962 Water Agreement

## The Cross‑Border Workforce

**Scale:** Approximately **350,000 Johor residents** cross into Singapore for work regularly, earning Singapore dollars (SGD). With the SGD/MYR exchange rate typically around 1 SGD = 3.2–3.5 MYR, this creates a significant income differential that shapes:

- **Property demand in JB** — cross‑border earners can afford higher JB property prices; this prices out local ringgit earners
- **Retail and food prices** — JB's proximity to Singapore inflates costs for non‑commuters
- **Political sensitivity to bilateral relations** — any disruption to Singapore‑Malaysia relations (visas, border rules, toll charges) is immediately felt by this large voter bloc

## Cross‑border Taxi Services

Starting **4 May 2026**, licensed taxis are permitted to drop off passengers anywhere in Singapore and at several designated locations in Johor, namely **Johor Bahru, Iskandar Puteri, Forest City, Kulai and Senai**. The policy is intended to streamline cross‑border commuting, boost tourism, and enhance commercial ties between the two cities. [Source: Free Malaysia Today, 2026-05-01]
