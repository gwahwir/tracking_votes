# Iskandar Malaysia

## Overview

**Iskandar Malaysia** is Malaysia's largest special economic zone (SEZ) by investment value, established in 2006 as the main southern development corridor in Johor. It spans **2,300 square kilometres** across three districts — Johor Bahru, Kulai, and part of Pontian — and has attracted over **RM413.1 billion in total investment**. [Source: Wikipedia – Iskandar Malaysia, 2024]

The zone is named after the current Yang di-Pertuan Agong (since January 2024) and former Sultan of Johor, Sultan Ibrahim Iskandar.

---

## Economic Scale

- **Total investment attracted:** Over RM413.1 billion (cumulative since 2006)
- **Area:** 2,300 km² (~3x the size of Singapore)
- **Population (2020):** Slightly over 2 million residents across five local government areas
  - Johor Bahru city: 858,118 people (largest component)
- **GDP contribution:** A major driver of Johor's economy and a significant contributor to national GDP

[Source: Wikipedia – Iskandar Malaysia, 2024]

---

## Five Economic Zones

| Zone | Location | Focus |
|------|----------|-------|
| Zone A | Johor Bahru City Centre | Financial services, retail, tourism |
| Zone B | Iskandar Puteri (formerly Nusajaya) | Government administration, education, healthcare |
| Zone C | Western Gate (Port of Tanjung Pelepas) | Logistics, port, industrial |
| Zone D | Eastern Gate (Johor Port, Pasir Gudang) | Petrochemical, heavy industry |
| Zone E | Senai-Skudai Corridor | Manufacturing, technology, airport |

[Source: Wikipedia – Iskandar Malaysia, 2024]

---

## Key Infrastructure

- Iskandar Coastal Highway
- Legoland Malaysia Resort (Zone B — tourism anchor)
- Kota Iskandar (new Johor state administrative capital, Zone B)
- Senai International Airport (Zone E)
- Port of Tanjung Pelepas (Zone C — one of Southeast Asia's busiest container ports)
- Johor Bahru–Singapore Rapid Transit System (RTS Link — under construction, see JB-Singapore Relations page)
- Multiple commuter rail stations and cargo
