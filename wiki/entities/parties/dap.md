# Democratic Action Party (DAP)

## Overview

The **Democratic Action Party (DAP)** — Parti Tindakan Demokratik — is a centre-left social democratic party founded on 11 October 1965 (legalised 18 March 1966). It emerged as the successor to the People's Action Party (PAP) of Singapore's Peninsular Malaysia branch after Singapore's separation from Malaysia in 1965. [Source: Wikipedia – Democratic Action Party, 2024]

DAP is the largest non-Malay opposition party in Malaysia and a key component of the **Pakatan Harapan (PH)** coalition. It draws its primary support base from urban and semi-urban Chinese-majority communities, though it explicitly positions itself as a multiracial party.

---

## Ideology

- **Social democracy** and centre-left politics
- **"Malaysian Malaysia"** — civic nationalism advocating equal rights for all citizens regardless of race or religion
- Opposes race-based affirmative action and Bumiputera-only privileges
- Champions parliamentary democracy, rule of law, anti-corruption
- Economic: progressive taxation, workers' rights, social safety net
- Multiracialism and inclusive governance

## Coalition Membership

DAP is a founding member of **Pakatan Harapan** (PH — Pact of Hope), formed in 2015. PH currently forms the federal government in coalition with BN and other parties (National Unity Government post-GE15). DAP holds ministerial positions in the current Anwar Ibrahim-led government. [Source: Wikipedia – Democratic Action Party, 2024]

## Johor Presence

DAP is the dominant opposition (now government coalition partner) in **urban Johor Bahru and surrounding constituencies**. Its strength reflects Johor's significant Chinese population (~35% of state population) concentrated in JB and Iskandar Puteri.

**Johor Parlimen seats held (GE15):**
- P156 Labis
- P158 Bakri
- P162 Kluang
- P165 Iskandar Puteri
- P166 Kulai

**2022 Johor State Election:**
- DAP won **10 of 56 DUN seats** — making it the second-largest party by seats despite heavy overall losses
- DAP's state seats are concentrated in Johor Bahru city, Skudai, and Iskandar Puteri urban corridors

[Source: Wikipedia – 2022 Johor state election, 2024]

**Key Johor figures:**

## Key Issues in Johor Context

- **Iskandar Malaysia development** — DAP advocates for equitable distribution of economic benefits and opposes displacement of lower-income communities
- **JB–Singapore relations** — commuter workforce rights; RTS Link; cost of living pressures from proximity to Singapore
- **Vernacular education** — defence of Chinese-medium schools (SJK(C)); key electoral issue for Chinese Johor voters
- **Housing affordability** — JB property prices driven up by Singapore-dollar purchasing power of cross-border workers
- **Anti-corruption** — DAP has consistently highlighted 1MDB and related scandals affecting BN credibility

---

## Voter Base Profile

DAP's Johor support comes primarily from:
- **Urban Chinese voters** in JB, Skudai, Kulai, Iskandar Puteri
- **Cross-border workers** (Johor residents working in Singapore) — estimated 100,000–150,000 who are sensitive to SGD/MYR exchange rates, transport costs, and bilateral relations
- **Young professionals** in Iskandar Malaysia economic zones
- Some Malay support in multi-ethnic constituencies where DAP fields Malay or Indian candidates
