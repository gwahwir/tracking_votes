# National Trust Party (Amanah) — Parti Amanah Negara

## Overview

**Parti Amanah Negara (Amanah)** — National Trust Party — is a progressive Islamist party founded on 16 September 2015 by a group of moderate Islamic leaders who broke away from PAS under the banner of **Gerakan Harapan Baru** (New Hope Movement). The party positions itself as a reformist Islamic alternative to PAS's more conservative and Hudud-focused approach. [Source: Wikipedia – National Trust Party (Malaysia), 2024]

Amanah is a component of the **Pakatan Harapan (PH)** coalition and holds ministerial positions in the current Anwar Ibrahim-led Unity Government.

---

## Ideology

- **Progressive Islamism** — Islamic ideals pursued within a liberal democratic, pluralist framework
- Rejects PAS's push for Hudud (Islamic criminal law) implementation
- Supports multiculturalism and inter-religious dialogue
- Champions social justice, anti-corruption, and institutional reform
- Positions itself as the moderate Islamic voice within PH, appealing to Malay Muslims uncomfortable with both UMNO's patronage politics and PAS's theocratic agenda

[Source: Wikipedia – National Trust Party (Malaysia), 2024]

---

## Founding Context

Amanah was created from the PAS breakaway following the 2015 PAS Muktamar (annual general assembly), when conservative factions took full control of PAS under Abdul Hadi Awang and passed a motion supporting Hudud. A group of reformists — including former PAS Deputy President **Mohamad Sabu** (Mat Sabu) and former ministers — left to form Amanah, arguing PAS had abandoned its social reform roots. [Source: Wikipedia – National Trust Party (Malaysia), 2024]

## Johor Presence

Amanah holds a modest but notable footprint in Johor, primarily in constituencies with significant Malay working-class and semi-urban populations.

**Johor Parlimen seats held:**
- P160 Sri Gading
- P163 Pulai

**2022 Johor State Election:**
- Amanah won **1 of 56 DUN seats**: N42 Simpang Jeram
- Contested as part of PH but suffered from BN's dominant Malay vote consolidation

## Key Issues in Johor Context

- **Islamic governance** — Amanah argues for a moderate, inclusive Islam in contrast to PAS's theocratic framing; relevant in Malay-majority Johor constituencies
- **Malay Muslim identity** — Amanah attempts to reassure Malay voters that PH is not anti-Malay or anti-Islam despite DAP's role in the coalition
- **Social equity** — advocates for needs-based (rather than race-based) assistance programmes
- **Anti-corruption** — consistent with PH's reform agenda; resonates in Johor where BN patronage networks are historically strong

---

## Voter Base Profile

- **Reform-minded Malay Muslims** who want Islamic values in governance but reject PAS's Hudud agenda
- **Former PAS supporters** who followed the 2015 reformist breakaway
- **Urban and semi-urban Malay professionals** comfortable with multiracial coalition politics
- Amanah is weakest where PAS is strongest (rural, religiously conservative areas of northern Johor)

---

## Relationship with PAS

Amanah and PAS share a common origin but are direct competitors for the Malay Muslim vote. PAS routinely accuses Amanah of being a "DAP agent" and compromising Islamic principles. Amanah counters that PAS has become an authoritarian theocratic party more interested in power than in genuine Islamic social reform. This rivalry shapes how Malay voters in Johor's mixed constituencies choose between PN and PH.
