# Parti Keadilan Rakyat (PKR) — People's Justice Party

## Overview

**Parti Keadilan Rakyat (PKR)** — People's Justice Party — is a multiracial reformist party founded on 4 April 1999 as the National Justice Party (Parti Keadilan Nasional) by **Wan Azizah Wan Ismail** and **Anwar Ibrahim**, following Anwar's sacking as Deputy Prime Minister and subsequent imprisonment by Mahathir Mohamad. The current entity was formed in 2003 through a merger with the Malaysian People's Party (Parti Rakyat Malaysia). [Source: Wikipedia – People's Justice Party (Malaysia), 2024]

PKR is the party of Prime Minister **Anwar Ibrahim**, who has led the party since 2008.

---

## Ideology

- **Reformism** — anti-corruption, clean governance, institutional reform
- **Social justice and economic justice** — reducing inequality, targeted assistance vs. race-based affirmative action
- **Multiracial democracy** — "a society that is just and a nation that is democratic, progressive and united"
- Secular democratic values with respect for Islam's constitutional role
- Primary support base: urban and semi-urban, mixed-ethnic constituencies

## Coalition Membership

PKR is a founder and the largest component of **Pakatan Harapan (PH)**. PKR President Anwar Ibrahim became Prime Minister following GE15, heading the National Unity Government in coalition with BN and other parties. [Source: Wikipedia – People's Justice Party (Malaysia), 2024]

## Johor Presence

PKR has a moderate but important presence in Johor, particularly in mixed-ethnic semi-urban constituencies. It serves as the Malay-inclusive face of the PH coalition in Johor, appealing to reform-minded Malay voters who are uncomfortable with DAP's Chinese-majority optics.

**Johor Parlimen seats (GE15 estimate):** PKR holds approximately 7 parliamentary seats in Johor. [STALE — last confirmed: 2024]

**2022 Johor State Election:**
- PKR won **1 of 56 DUN seats** — a sharp drop reflecting Malay voter swing to BN
- PKR contested in multi-ethnic and semi-urban seats where Malay and mixed electorates exist

**Key Johor constituencies for PKR:**
- Semi-urban seats in Johor Bahru district (Skudai corridor)
- Mixed constituencies in Kluang and Batu Pahat districts

## Key Issues in Johor Context

- **Institutional reform** — electoral reform, judiciary independence; resonates with educated urban voters
- **Anti-corruption** — PKR's founding cause; relevant against BN incumbents tainted by 1MDB
- **Cost of living** — particularly acute for JB residents who earn in ringgit but face Singapore-proximity pricing
- **Undi18** — PKR actively courted newly registered youth voters in GE15 and the 2022 Johor state election
- **Cross-border labour** — Johor commuters working in Singapore; PKR has advocated for bilateral labour agreements

---

## Voter Base Profile

- **Reform-minded Malay voters** in semi-urban areas dissatisfied with UMNO/BN
- **Multi-ethnic urban voters** who see PKR as a bridge party between DAP and Malay parties
- **Youth voters** energised by Undi18 and anti-establishment sentiment
- **Civil society** and professional class
