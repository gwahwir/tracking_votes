# Barisan Nasional (BN) / United Malays National Organisation (UMNO)

## Overview

**Barisan Nasional (BN)** — National Front — is a multi-ethnic conservative coalition that governed Malaysia federally from 1974 until its historic defeat in GE14 (2018). It is the successor to the Alliance Party formed after the 13 May 1969 racial riots. [Source: Wikipedia – Barisan Nasional, 2024]

**United Malays National Organisation (UMNO)** is BN's anchor and largest component party. Founded on 11 May 1946 by Onn Jaafar, UMNO is Malaysia's oldest national party. It was declared unlawful in 1988 and re-registered as UMNO Baru on 13 February 1988. [Source: Wikipedia – United Malays National Organisation, 2024]

---

## Ideology

- **Ketuanan Melayu** (Malay supremacy / Malay primacy) — defends Bumiputera privileges enshrined in the Federal Constitution
- National conservatism and social conservatism
- Defends Islam's constitutional position as the religion of the federation
- Communal representation model: BN comprises separate parties for Malays (UMNO), Chinese (MCA), and Indians (MIC)

[Source: Wikipedia – United Malays National Organisation, 2024]

## Coalition Membership

BN was Malaysia's ruling coalition for 44 years (1974–2018). After the worst result in its history in GE15 (2022), BN joined a Pakatan Harapan-led **National Unity Government**, with UMNO accepting the Deputy Prime Minister role. This represented a historic reversal — BN and PH were bitter rivals in GE14. [Source: Wikipedia – Barisan Nasional, 2024]

**BN component parties (post-GE15):**
- UMNO (26 of 30 BN parliamentary seats)
- Malaysian Chinese Association / MCA (2 seats)
- Malaysian Indian Congress / MIC (1 seat)
- United Sabah People's Party / PBRS (1 seat)

## Johor Presence

Johor is historically one of UMNO/BN's most important strongholds — sometimes called "UMNO's fortress." The Johor royal family has historically maintained close ties with UMNO leadership.

**2022 Johor State Election:** BN won a landslide **40 of 56 DUN seats**, regaining dominance lost in GE14.
- UMNO: 33 seats
- MCA: 4 seats
- MIC: 3 seats

[Source: Wikipedia – 2022 Johor state election, 2024]

**Key Johor figures:**
- **Hasni Mohammad** — former Johor Menteri Besar (Chief Minister), UMNO; led BN back to dominance in the 2022 state election
- **Mohamed Khaled Nordin** — UMNO Vice President; previously Johor Menteri Besar (2013–2018)

## Key Issues in Johor

- Defence of Bumiputera economic rights and special privileges
- Malay unity — competition with Bersatu/PN for Malay vote share
- Development of Iskandar Malaysia and Johor-Singapore economic corridor
- Water supply agreement with Singapore (Johor supplies Singapore water under a 1962 agreement expiring 2061)
- Cross-border workers: ~350,000 daily Causeway crossers, many Johor residents working in Singapore

---

## Relationship with Johor Sultanate

The Johor royal family has historically maintained an assertive political role distinct from other Malaysian royals. The Sultan of Johor has publicly commented on governance and development matters. BN/UMNO's historically close relationship with the palace has been a source of political legitimacy in Johor. [Source: Wikipedia – United Malays National Organisation, 2024]
